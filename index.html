An Agricultural Soil Classifier Utilizing YOLO in TensorFlow Framework

One of the first steps in cultivating the soil for healthy and productive crops is to specify its texture (Arcuri, 2021). Soil texture, an essential physical characteristic, is significant to determine as it influences many aspects of soil quality (Stivers, 2023). One can determine soil texture by classifying it conventionally by hand, but doing so requires an amount of competence, and results could vary based on experience (Backus, n.d.). Therefore, the proponents chose a contemporary strategy as innovation in recognition of the importance of this procedure.


With technology gradually emerging in agriculture, the proponents propose a prototype whose primary functionality is automating soil texture classification by leveraging machine learning capabilities with computer vision. It aims to achieve satisfactory accuracy capable of revolutionizing the established methods, the Soil Texture Feel Chart and the Soil Texture Triangle, while using these as guides. Additionally, the proponents plan to add a crop suggestion system as an additional feature. This component will further expand the device's purpose to farming or planting other than soil information gathering.


This creative approach strives to close the gap between conventional practices and modern innovations in agriculture. Essentially, a new era of precision agriculture, where well-informed decisions contribute to higher crop output and sustainable practices, may be ushered in better by the marriage of soil science and machine learning.
